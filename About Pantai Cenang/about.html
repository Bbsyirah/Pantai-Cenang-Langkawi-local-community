BACKGROUND INFORMATIONS

Pantai Cenang is located on the opposite side on Rebak Island an it is one of the beaches in Langkawi that are popular as an attraction places among tourists to visit. A 2.2 kilometers long tourists corridor called Pantai Cenang, was first built in the 1980s. In Langkawi, Pantai Cenang is a “destination-mix” that offers pristine tropical beaches as a key tourism offering. Although the accommodations and beachside activities are really a byproduct of this destination. The Paddy Field Complex, which includes the Paddy Museum component, paddy fields, and restaurants in 1998, and Underwater World, a park with the largest man-made aquarium and marine in Asia, were both developed as secondary attractions to enhance the value of recreational and leisure-based activities currently based on existing coastal resources.

A total of 57.77 hectares of land were developed as a tourism enterprise area as of December 2010. The corridor Pantai Cenang offers 1,883 rooms which includes about 35 hotels, resort, and chalets with varying star ratings plus with a capacity of 3,766 guest per hour. Additionally, Pantai Cenang is well-known for hosting such gatherings. Tourism Malaysia organizes the three-day Langkawi Water Festival and Langkawi Water Stage events as part of the country’s annual tourism calendar.
